I read this after already running 0waste as a spreadsheet for a few months, which turned out to be the right order — the book's ideas landed as confirmation and vocabulary for things I'd stumbled into, rather than as untested theory.

## What held up in practice

The Build-Measure-Learn loop is genuinely how the first months of a real business feel, once you stop resisting it. The instinct to build the "real" version before testing the assumption is strong and almost always wrong. Ries is convincing on this because he's specific about failure modes, not just the theory.

## What felt dated

Some of the specific tooling references and case studies feel of their era now, and a few of the "pivot" examples read as survivorship-biased in hindsight. The underlying framework holds up better than the case studies chosen to illustrate it.

## Who should read this

Anyone about to spend six months building something nobody's validated wants to exist. It's a fast, occasionally repetitive read, but the repetition is doing real work — the core loop is worth over-learning.

This is the book I recommend most often, and the one I re-read most often — not because I forget the ideas, but because I forget to apply them. My full working notes are in [my separate summary post](/blog/atomic-habits-summary), but here's the short review.

## Why it works

Clear doesn't invent new psychology so much as he compresses genuinely good behavioral science into a framework simple enough to actually use under stress, which is the only condition habits are ever really tested under. The identity-based reframe — "I'm a person who writes daily" instead of "I'm trying to write more" — is the single idea from this book I use the most, a year later.

## Where it's weaker

Light on what happens when a habit stops serving you. The framework is much better at building than at un-building, and real change often requires both.

## Would I read it again

Yes, and I do, about once a year. Few productivity books earn a re-read. This one does.
